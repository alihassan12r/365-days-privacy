Privacy policy

Days

Last updated: 21 May 2026

Days is built around one idea: open the app, see how much of the year is gone, close it. That principle extends to your data — there is none to collect, because the app does not need any.

  • Data collectedNone.
  • AnalyticsNone.
  • TrackingNone.
  • Network callsNone. The app works fully offline.
  • AccountsNone. No sign-up, no login.
  • AdsNone.
  • Third-party SDKsNone for data collection.

What the app does on your device

Days reads the current date from your device and computes how many days of the year have passed. That calculation happens entirely on-device. The contribution grid you see is derived from one number — today's date — and nothing else.

Permissions

Days requests no runtime permissions. The Android build explicitly blocks INTERNET, ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE, and related permissions in the manifest — the app cannot make network calls even if a future bug tried.

Children

Days does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13. There is no information to collect.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated date at the top of this page will change with it. Substantive changes will be reflected in a new app release.
